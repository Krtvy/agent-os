## PERSONA 1 — THE JOINT FAMILY
**"The Sharma Household, South Delhi"**

### Who they are
```
Members:     6-8 people across 2-3 generations
             Dadi (72), Dada (75)
             Dad (48), Mom (44) — both working
             Son (19), Daughter (16)
             Sometimes: Bua / Mausi visiting

City:        Delhi NCR, Bangalore, Pune
Income:      ₹25-60 lakh/household/year
Home:        3-4 BHK, owned
```

### The dynamics
- **Dadi runs the kitchen** — even if she doesn't cook, she decides the menu
- Dada is strict vegetarian + Tuesday-Thursday fast
- Mom is working (IT company), leaves at 8:30 AM
- Dad travels 2 weeks/month for work
- Kids have Western food preferences (pasta, sandwiches) which nobody else wants
- The cook (Sunita, been with them 7 years) knows all this — but she's the single point of failure

### Dietary complexity: **VERY HIGH**
```
Dadi/Dada:   Strict vegetarian, no onion-garlic on fasting days,
             Navratri = 9 days sattvic, Ekadashi = no grains
Mom/Dad:     Non-vegetarian but prefer veg at home for ease
Son:         Non-veg, wants chicken 3x/week, eats anything
Daughter:    Trying to go vegan (current phase)
Guests:      Always possible — Bua is Jain
```

### Meal patterns
```
Breakfast:   Dal paratha / Poha / Upma (Dadi's preference)
             Son: sometimes just cereal if late
Lunch:       Full thali — dal + 2 sabzis + roti + rice + raita
             Dada eats at home (retired), gets elaborate lunch
Dinner:      Lighter — khichdi / rotis + sabzi / soup
             Festival nights: elaborate halwa-puri spread
Cooking:     Cook comes 8 AM - 12 PM, sometimes evenings
             Mom cooks on weekends
```

### Pain points
- Managing 4+ dietary requirement sets simultaneously
- Cook falls sick during festivals (peak demand) — no backup
- Guest arrivals are last-minute ("Bua is coming for 3 days")
- Dadi's instructions to cook via phone create confusion
- Son wants chicken, Dadi doesn't want it cooked in the house

### What M solves for them
- Single "family meal plan" that respects all dietary restrictions
- Festival mode: auto-switches to Navratri menu for 9 days
- Guest mode: "2 guests for dinner, they're Jain" → instant menu adjustment
- Dadi can add instructions via WhatsApp that M relays to cook properly

## PERSONA 2 — TWO BACHELORS
**"Rohan & Arjun's Flat, Koramangala Bangalore"**

### Who they are
```
Members:     2 people
             Rohan (26) — software engineer, non-veg
             Arjun (27) — designer, vegetarian

City:        Bangalore, Mumbai, Gurgaon, Hyderabad
Income:      ₹8-18 LPA each, ₹16-36 LPA combined
Home:        2 BHK rented flat, 50:50 split
```

### The dynamics
- Both spend ₹8,000-12,000/month on Zomato/Swiggy each
- Fridge contains: beer, old curd, leftover pizza, 2 onions
- Neither wants to cook but both feel guilty about the Zomato spending
- They have a maid who comes 3x/week for cleaning — doesn't cook
- "We should start cooking" — said every Sunday, acted on never
- The 3 days/month someone actually cooks: everyone's happy

### Dietary complexity: **LOW**
```
Rohan:  Non-veg, no restrictions, eats everything
Arjun:  Vegetarian, no onion-garlic (sometimes — not strict)
        Actually eats egg, identifies as "eggetarian"
Both:   No fasting, no religious restrictions
```

### Meal patterns
```
Breakfast:   Usually skipped (9:30 AM WFH start)
             Sometimes: toast + eggs, or just coffee
Lunch:       Order Swiggy (₹300-450/order each)
             Or mess/canteen if going to office
Dinner:      Order Swiggy/Zomato 4-5 days/week
             Cook 2-3 days: dal-chawal, maggi-with-extras, eggs
Weekend:     Sunday brunch = elaborate cooking (pasta, biryani)
             Saturday = hangover + more Zomato
Cooking:     No dedicated cook
             Self-cook when motivated / out of money
```

### Pain points
- ₹15,000-20,000/month combined on food delivery = guilt
- Grocery shopping: buy ingredients → forget to cook → ingredients rot
- Rohan wants chicken, Arjun won't touch it (shared cooking impossible)
- Splitting grocery bills is awkward — who bought what
- Sunday meal prep sounds like a good idea every Saturday night

### What M solves for them
- **"This week's ₹500 cooking challenge"** — 5 meals under ₹500 total
- Ingredient list that won't go to waste if they cook only 3/7 days
- Separate meal sections: Rohan's non-veg ideas, Arjun's veg ideas
- Weekly report: "You spent ₹12,000 on Swiggy this week. 3 home meals would've cost ₹400."
- Dead-simple recipes: "10-minute dal chawal" for a Wednesday night

## PERSONA 3 — SINGLE PARENT + CHILD
**"Priya & Aarav, Powai Mumbai"**

### Who they are
```
Members:     2 people
             Priya (36) — product manager, divorced
             Aarav (8) — class 3, fussy eater

City:        Mumbai, Delhi, Bangalore
Income:      ₹20-40 LPA
Home:        2 BHK rented, near good school
```

### The dynamics
- Priya leaves at 8:30 AM, back by 7:30 PM
- School tiffin needs to be packed by 7:45 AM every day
- Aarav eats: rice, paneer, curd, roti — that's basically it
- Won't eat: anything green, bitter gourd, karela, dal (sometimes)
- Priya eats healthy, counts calories, avoids fried food
- Cook (Kamla) comes 7 AM - 10 AM daily, makes breakfast + pack lunch
- Priya cooks dinner after getting home, exhausted

### Dietary complexity: **MEDIUM**
```
Priya:   Vegetarian, health-conscious, avoids oil/fried
         Intermittent fasting some days (no breakfast)
Aarav:   Vegetarian, VERY FUSSY
         Will eat: paneer butter masala, curd rice, plain dal,
                   roti with butter, maggi, pizza
         Won't eat: anything "green", karela, lauki, bitter flavors
```

### Meal patterns
```
Breakfast (Aarav 7:30 AM):
  Mon: Poha
  Tue: Paratha with curd
  Wed: Idli-sambar (Kamla knows the routine)
  Thu: Upma (Aarav complains every week)
  Fri: Eggs or cereal (Priya's lazy Friday)

School Tiffin (packed 7:45 AM):
  Needs to be: non-messy, no curry, finger food preferred
  Aarav likes: sandwich, paratha roll, fruits, curd rice
  School rule: no junk food (Priya's problem every day)

Lunch:
  Priya: office canteen or delivered salad
  Aarav: school lunch + tiffin

Dinner (8 PM, post-bedtime rush):
  Priya cooks: wants something quick, under 30 min
  Aarav's bedtime is 9:30 — dinner at 8 PM sharp
  Often: dal-chawal (easy), paneer dish, or Swiggy on late nights
```

### Pain points
- **School tiffin**: every night before bed, "what do I pack tomorrow"
- Aarav's fussiness means Priya cooks separately for him sometimes
- Running out of things for Aarav to eat on short notice
- Priya's healthy goals vs. Aarav's processed food preferences
- Too exhausted at 7:30 PM to think about dinner, let alone make it
- Grocery shopping on weekends = the one window, often misses items

### What M solves for them
- **School tiffin planner**: 5-day tiffin rotation that Aarav will actually eat
- Separate dinner recipes under 30 minutes (for tired-evening Priya)
- "Aarav-approved" recipe tag — only shows what he'll eat
- Nutrition report: "Aarav got 0 vegetables this week"
- Weekly grocery list that accounts for both their very different needs

## PERSONA 4 — THREE GIRLS SHARING A FLAT
**"Anika, Rhea & Meghna, Baner Pune"**

### Who they are
```
Members:     3 people
             Anika (25) — UX designer, vegetarian
             Rhea (26) — marketing manager, non-veg
             Meghna (24) — data analyst, "trying to eat healthy"

City:        Pune, Bangalore, Hyderabad, Noida
Income:      ₹6-14 LPA each
Home:        3 BHK rented, equal split
```

### The dynamics
- All three have different work schedules and meal times
- Grocery is split equally but someone always feels they're buying more
- Cooking rotation: whoever's home earliest makes dinner (mostly Meghna)
- Sunday meal prep has happened exactly twice in 6 months
- Anika and Rhea's food preferences rarely overlap (veg vs non-veg)
- Meghna is on a health kick: keto last month, intermittent fasting this month
- The WhatsApp group "Flat 302 Food" is 90% "what's for dinner" and "who ordered veg"

### Dietary complexity: **MEDIUM-HIGH**
```
Anika:   Strict vegetarian, no egg
         Health-conscious, portion-controlled
Rhea:    Non-veg, loves chicken/fish
         No restrictions, will eat anything
Meghna:  Non-veg but "trying to be healthy"
         Currently: intermittent fasting (eating window 12-8 PM)
         Avoids: refined sugar, maida, fried food (this week)
         Next week: all bets off
Budget:  Very conscious — each person's food budget ₹4,000-6,000/month
```

### Meal patterns
```
Breakfast:
  Usually individual: Anika = smoothie, Rhea = toast+eggs, Meghna = nothing (fasting)
  Weekend: elaborate brunch together (pancakes, shakshuka)

Lunch:
  All three: office canteen or packed
  Rhea: client lunches 3x/week
  Anika packs lunch 4 days/week (health reasons)

Dinner (the main coordination point):
  Mon: Whoever's home early cooks (Meghna usually)
  Tue: Anika cooks vegetarian (Rhea orders chicken separately)
  Wed: Order Swiggy (split by what each orders)
  Thu: Rhea's turn, makes non-veg (Anika orders separately)
  Fri: Everyone's out or orders individually
  Sat: Big cook-together — biryani or pasta (rare luxury)
  Sun: Meal prep attempt (rarely successful)

Cook:     No dedicated cook
          Cleaning maid 6 days/week (doesn't cook)
```

### Pain points
- **The "whose turn to cook" problem** — tracking, guilt, resentment
- Can't make one meal that works for all three dietary needs
- Grocery splitting: who bought the expensive olive oil? Who owes what?
- Food waste: buying for 3 with different preferences, half gets thrown out
- Meghna's diet changes weekly — hard to plan around
- Ordering separately for non-veg vs veg adds up fast
- No system for "what's in the fridge" — duplicate buying

### What M solves for them
- **Cooking rotation tracker**: whose turn tonight, what they made last time
- Separate veg + non-veg dinner suggestions (Anika cooks veg, Rhea adds chicken)
- Grocery splitting: M tracks who bought what, settle weekly
- Pantry tracker: "You have 3 tomatoes, half a capsicum, and 100g paneer — make this"
- **Health mode toggle** for Meghna: switches her meal suggestions automatically
- Budget mode: "This week's 3-person dinner under ₹800 total"

## PERSONA 5 — THE SOLO BACHELOR/BACHELORETTE
**"Kartavya, Koramangala Studio, Bangalore"**

### Who they are
```
Members:     1 person
             Kartavya (23) — fresher at tech company / freelancer
             OR: Shreya (29) — senior manager, lives alone by choice

City:        Bangalore, Mumbai, Delhi, Hyderabad, Pune
Income:      ₹6-25 LPA
Home:        Studio / 1 BHK rented, solo
```

### The dynamics
- **Kartavya version**: just moved out of hostel/PG, first time living alone
  - Doesn't know how to cook beyond maggi and chai
  - Orders Zomato 2x/day, spending ₹15,000+/month on food
  - Grocery shopping = stressful, buys random things, nothing goes together
  - 90% of home-cooked attempts: rice + dal (only dish known well)
  - Health is deteriorating but doesn't have bandwidth to think about it

- **Shreya version**: lives alone by choice, health-conscious, wants to cook
  - Knows basics, wants meal variety but cooking for 1 = waste
  - Goes to gym 4x/week, tracks macros (loosely)
  - Grocery shopping: buys 500g chicken, uses 200g, rest goes bad
  - Plans to meal prep Sunday, actually watches Netflix instead

### Dietary complexity: **LOW**
```
Kartavya: Non-veg (or veg), no restrictions
          No religious fasting
          Basically: "I'll eat anything edible"

Shreya:  Non-veg, health-conscious
         Tracks: protein (80g/day goal), calories (1600/day)
         Avoids: heavy oil, refined carbs, late-night eating
         Loves: salads, grilled chicken, dal, fruit
```

### Meal patterns
```
Breakfast:
  Often skipped (Kartavya)
  Protein shake + fruit (Shreya — health mode)

Lunch:
  Office canteen or order Swiggy
  Shreya sometimes packs lunch (Sunday meal prep guilt)

Dinner:
  Kartavya: Zomato 5x/week, maggi 1x/week, rice+dal 1x/week
  Shreya: Tries to cook 4-5x/week; sometimes fails
  Both: biggest waste of money and health happens here

Cook:     None
          Sometimes: maid for cleaning, not cooking
Grocery:  Occasional BigBasket order, often wrong quantities
          Things bought for "planned" cooking sit unused
```

### Pain points
- **Cooking for 1 = terrible math**: half a cauliflower, quarter a cabbage
- Grocery quantities don't scale to 1 person (smallest pack is "for 3-4")
- Buying perishables, forgetting to cook, throwing them away
- No variety — rotates between 4 dishes they know how to make
- Health anxiety: "I'm only eating Zomato junk and I know it"
- Meal prep intention vs. Netflix reality gap (every Sunday)
- No one to share grocery cost or cooking effort

### What M solves for them
- **"Cooking for 1" grocery quantities**: exact amounts, no waste
- **Beginner cook mode**: only 15-minute recipes with 5 ingredients max
- **Weekly "cook once, eat twice" plans**: batch cooking suggestions
- "Your Zomato equivalent would cost ₹X — these ingredients cost ₹Y"
- Protein/calorie tracking built into meal plan (Shreya version)
- **"Fridge rescue"** feature: "I have eggs, tomato, and bread — make this"

## HOW THIS CHANGES ONBOARDING

**Question 1 of 5 during signup:**

```
What kind of household are you?

[ ] Joint family (3+ generations, 5+ people)
[ ] Two people sharing (flatmates/couple/bachelors)
[ ] Single parent with child(ren)
[ ] Group flatmates (3+ people sharing)
[ ] Living alone
```

**This one answer changes:**
- Meal plan complexity (1 person vs 8)
- Dietary setup flow (1 diet profile vs 4-6)
- Cook communication (yes/no)
- Grocery quantities
- Budget tracking (split vs individual)
- Feature defaults (tiffin planner for Persona 3, cost splitter for Persona 4)
