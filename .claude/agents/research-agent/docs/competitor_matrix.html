D2 synthesis: Three patterns dominate winners. (1) Default-to-subscription PDPs — every Tier-C brand and most Tier-A do this; many smaller brands invert it and lose 30%+ AOV. (2) Bundle-anchor over single-SKU as primary entry — Goli, MaryRuth's, Bloom, AG1, Momentous all anchor on multi-pack/bundle PDPs. (3) Quiz funnel adoption is split: Hims/Hers, Bloom, Magic Mind, MaryRuth's, Calm yes; AG1, Ritual, Olly no. Quiz advantage is largest where personalization meaningfully changes the recommendation; for a 3-SKU brand like Rootlab (Magnesium / Ashwagandha / Alpha GPC), quiz is high-leverage because each customer maps cleanly to one stack.

D3 synthesis: The premium-positioned brands (AG1, Ritual, Seed, Momentous, Moon Juice) are discount-light by design; they preserve price integrity and use loyalty + sub mechanics + news-cycle moments as the alternative to flash sales. D4 synthesis: The most powerful habit primitives in the dataset are structural format choices, not lifecycle tactics: Nello/Magic Mind's single-serve sachets/shots eliminate measurement friction; Bloom's branded shaker turns consumption into UGC; Seed's refillable jar makes the artifact daily-visible; Calm/Hims-Hers attach the supplement to a non-supplement habit. For Rootlab, the highest-leverage habit move is a deliberate "ritual artifact" — branded gummy jar designed to live counter-side, daily-dose markers on the bottle, travel sachet line — combined with Olly's benefit-first naming + AG1's "foundational daily habit" identity language.
