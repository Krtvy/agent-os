## D1 — Creator Program & Affiliate Design

- **Seed University:** 59-minute training program covering microbiome science + probiotic mechanism + FTC compliance. Every influencer partner must **pass an exam** before promotion. Three jobs: (1) screens out unwilling-to-engage creators, (2) creates network of scientifically literate advocates, (3) ensures consistent FTC-compliant messaging at scale.
- **CPA-disciplined influencer program:** strict CPA targets per influencer; partners cut if they don't hit benchmarks.
- **Distinctive:** Seed treats influencer onboarding as a structured education-and-credentialing process, not a sample-and-hope model. The cost is upfront friction; the return is brand integrity at scale.

## D2 — Site & App Teardown

- **Refillable packaging system:** compostable refill packs + reusable dark glass jar — habit-engagement asset (the jar lives on the counter; refills perpetuate engagement).
- Education-heavy PDP: clinical trial references, ingredient mechanism, microbiome explainer.
- Subscription default; AI-personalization rolling out via Auggi acquisition (gut-health diagnostics app).

## D3 — Deals & Campaign Cadence

- Discount-light; brand integrity over promotional velocity.
- Target launch (Sep 2024) used **exclusive product** (DS-01 14-Day Gut Reset) to avoid DTC channel conflict.
- Sprouts launch (Jun 2025) — 450 stores nationally.
- **Marketing tactics include the Gwyneth Paltrow + Times Square viral deer activation** (2024) — unconventional cultural-conversation play for a science brand.

## D4 — Habit-Change Tactics

- **Refillable jar is a habit primitive.** The jar (not just the supplement) becomes the daily artifact — refilling it is a brand re-engagement event, not a generic reorder.
- Education-as-retention: continued teaching post-purchase deepens commitment.
- "Planetary health" mission (probiotics for honeybees + coral reefs) attracts mission-aligned higher-LTV customers.

## What Rootlab should steal

1. **Build a "Rootlab University" credentialing program for top creators.** A 30-minute training + simple exam covering magnesium/ashwagandha mechanism + FTC compliance. Creators who pass get tier upgrades (commission %, exclusive drops, sample priority). This converts the credibility playbook (MaryRuth's) into a structural moat (Seed's).
2. **Refillable / reusable bottle system for the perimenopause-night SKU.** A premium ceramic-or-glass jar that lives on the bedside table + monthly compostable refills. Higher upfront cost, higher first-purchase margin pressure, but the jar becomes a daily artifact and an Instagram-able ritual surface.
3. **CPA discipline as the only metric that matters in influencer.** Cut creators who don't hit CPA inside 60 days. Rootlab's affiliate program almost certainly carries low-performing long-tail creators; pruning them improves contribution margin.

## What Rootlab should NOT copy

- **Seed's "tone."** Highly clinical, slightly chilly. Rootlab's audience (perimenopause sleep-anxious women) wants warmth + relatability, not lab-coat language.
