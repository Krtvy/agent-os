## D1 — Creator Program & Affiliate Design

- **Primary channel is podcast sponsorship**, not creator volume. ~$50M/year in audio sponsorships [T3].
- **Tiered influencer ecosystem:** Authority Figures (Huberman, Attia) → Performance Voices (athletes/coaches) → Lifestyle Navigators (IG/TikTok creators).
- **Trackable URLs from day one** — every show gets a unique URL; unprofitable shows cut within a week.
- **Tim Ferriss as founding-era moment**: one mention in 2011 → 10× monthly sales spike (500 → 5,000 tubs in 8 weeks).
- **Distinctive:** AG1 is the inverse of the Tier-A volume-creator playbook. Ultra-curated, ultra-premium, podcast-led. The lesson is the _trackable-attribution discipline_, not the channel itself.

## D2 — Site & App Teardown

- Subscribe & Save is the primary purchase path (not single bottle) — within days of 2012 launch, 70% of new buyers chose recurring.
- Welcome kit on first delivery: branded canister + shaker + travel sachets (the "Essentials Kit" — 2019 retention play).
- Ships 30-day supply on the canister cadence (built-in reorder rhythm).
- "Foundational Daily Habit" as the brand framing across all PDP copy.
- 2025 SMS subscription management rollout: one-click sub management → ~15% churn reduction.

## D3 — Deals & Campaign Cadence

- Subscription discount is the only consistent discount; brand avoids broad sales.
- **2023 "Foundational Daily Habit" campaign** repositioned AG1 from corrective supplement to morning ritual → +40% brand search; lowered avg customer age by 5 years.
- **2025 "AG1: Anywhere" campaign** with luxury airline + hotel placements → 500M+ social impressions, +25% travel-pack sales.
- Travel sachets are a deliberate ritual-continuity SKU (overlap with Forest Fresh Honey insight — empty-jar prevention).

## D4 — Habit-Change Tactics

- **The morning ritual reposition is the masterclass.** Reframed AG1 from "corrective" to "foundational daily habit" — pure identity-framing language at scale.
- Welcome kit is intentional first-month UX engineering — brand-canister + shaker establish the ritual artifacts.
- AI-driven behavioral email flows segment users into "Life-State" cohorts (busy professional, aging athlete, wellness parent) → +12% 90-day retention.
- **CLV ~4.5× CAC** [T3].

## What Rootlab should steal

1. **The "Foundational Daily Habit" framing language.** "Take care of your nervous system every day" or "the foundation of how you sleep" — anchoring MagAshwa as foundational vs. corrective is identity-coded language and lifts retention.
2. **Trackable URLs from day one** for every podcast/influencer touchpoint. AG1's CAC discipline is a function of attribution discipline. Rootlab should clone this hygiene.
3. **Ritual-artifact kit on first delivery.** A branded Rootlab "wind-down kit" (gummy bottle + tracker card + travel sachets + branded sleep journal) on first delivery converts the purchase into a ritual event.

## What Rootlab should NOT copy

- **The $90/month price point.** AG1's premium pricing requires the podcast-credibility moat which Rootlab can't replicate at small scale. Rootlab's pricing should sit in TikTok-Shop impulse range ($30–60).
