## D1 — Creator Program & Affiliate Design

- **Influencer network used to drive _retail_ sales** — not just DTC. Followers told to "pick up at Target" or "find at Walmart." Bridges social discovery and physical retail.
- **2025 "Unlicensed Shower Therapy" campaign** with Heather McMahan (Mood + Skin launch) — comedian + social-first humor.
- **Distinctive:** Olly uses creators as _retail traffic drivers_, not as DTC conversion engines — the inverse of Tier A's TikTok-Shop closed-loop. This is what Rootlab will need to learn the moment it enters retail.

## D2 — Site & App Teardown

- **Benefit-first product naming:** "Sleep" not "Melatonin 5mg," "Immunity" not "Vitamin C 1000mg." Removes purchase friction at retail browsing.
- **Bold visual branding:** colorful, friendly, anti-clinical — accessible-wellness aesthetic.
- DTC site is a learning lab — used to test messaging and gather data before retail rollout.
- **Receipt scanning loyalty program:** customers buying at Target/Walmart can scan receipts → first-party data on in-store purchasers → enables lifecycle marketing to retail-only customers. The single most underused tactic in omnichannel supplements.
- CRO results (Contentsquare): +20% website revenue MoM; +91% YoY; +3% AOV.

## D3 — Deals & Campaign Cadence

- Retail-shelf-driven (price stability matters); DTC promotions stay within retail-friendly bands.
- Mood + Skin launch (Jun 2025) — $12.99 at Target/Walmart/Amazon/DTC simultaneously. Personal-care extension.
- **Mood health search interest +61.9% YoY** (Google + TikTok combined) is the trend that justified the personal-care expansion.

## D4 — Habit-Change Tactics

- **Benefit-first naming is identity-coded.** "I take Sleep" is identity-framed; "I take 5mg melatonin" is mechanism-framed. The naming itself drives habit adoption faster.
- Email + SMS segmentation avoids list-blasting — relevance over volume.
- DTC-as-learning-lab feeds product/messaging insights to retail (long retention loop).
- "Psycare" category positioning (2025 Mood + Skin) bridges wellness and beauty in a way most supplements don't.

## What Rootlab should steal

1. **Benefit-first naming for the SKU line.** Rename Magnesium → "Sleep," Ashwagandha → "Calm," Alpha GPC → "Focus" (or stack-coded equivalents) on PDPs and TikTok Shop listings. Customers shop by outcome at point of impulse, not by ingredient.
2. **Receipt-scanning loyalty (when retail launches).** The moment Rootlab is in any retail door, a receipt-scan loyalty program captures retail customers into the lifecycle CRM that would otherwise be black-box. Olly's tactic is a 1-app integration ($150–500/month) and high-leverage.
3. **DTC-as-learning-lab discipline.** Test all new claims, bundles, and pricing on the DTC site first; only roll to retail what wins. Olly's most operationally replicable habit.

## What Rootlab should NOT copy

- **Mass-retail-from-day-one positioning.** Olly is in 100K+ retail doors. Rootlab is TikTok-first; chasing mass retail too early sacrifices the channel-volume creator engine that's currently working.
- **The Unilever back-end.** Olly's category expansion benefits from Unilever's supply chain + research budgets — not replicable independently.
