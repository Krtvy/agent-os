## Standout tactic worth stealing

**Quiz funnel as the universal acquisition front-door.** Every Hims/Hers product entry begins with a structured 5–8 question quiz. Quiz completers are 4.3× annual spend (per industry benchmarks) and the quiz captures all the zero-party data that powers downstream lifecycle. Hims/Hers has this dialed at scale across multiple categories — Rootlab's quiz funnel design (per the Phase-1 growth-playbook recommendation) should benchmark against theirs.

## Four-dimension quick read

- **D1 (creator program):** Minimal creator-volume play; primary acquisition via Meta + Google + brand search. This is the contrast brand to Tier A.
- **D2 (site/app):** Subscription-default everything; quiz funnel is the universal entry point; native app for prescription management that doubles as a habit-tracking surface for supplements.
- **D3 (deals):** Bundle pricing within the telehealth subscription; supplements priced as a "small attach" to the prescription subscription.
- **D4 (habits):** **App is the habit moat.** Customers manage their telehealth and supplements in the same app — supplement adherence rides on top of prescription adherence (much higher baseline). This is the most defensible habit primitive in the dataset and not replicable without a telehealth backbone.

## What Rootlab should steal

1. **Quiz funnel design discipline.** Use Hims/Hers' quiz as the gold-standard reference for question phrasing, results-page reasoning ("based on your answers"), and the soft handoff to a recommended bundle. Do not invent the quiz structure — clone it.
2. **Default-to-subscription PDP architecture.** Single-purchase is the _secondary_ option on Hims/Hers PDPs, not the primary. Most supplement brands invert this. Rootlab should test default-sub.

## What Rootlab should NOT copy

- **The telehealth-attach model.** Rootlab doesn't have a prescription business and shouldn't invent one. The app-as-habit-moat works for Hims/Hers because the prescription is already there; trying to launch an app for supplements alone usually fails (ROI not proven for SKU < $100/mo in supplement category).
