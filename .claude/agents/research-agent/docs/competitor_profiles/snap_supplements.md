## What Rootlab should steal from SNAP

1. **The "creators filming in retail stores" tactic.** Once Rootlab is in _any_ retail door (even a single Sprouts test), brief creators to film themselves discovering MagAshwa on the shelf. The content does double duty: TikTok views + drives in-store visits + signals retail legitimacy to the next retailer in the pipeline.
2. **Retail expansion as an explicit 12–18 month milestone.** SNAP shows the path from TikTok proof-of-concept to 8,000 doors in under three years is real, not hypothetical. Rootlab should bake retail into the playbook timeline (Phase 4 in the growth playbook), not treat it as "someday."
3. **Category dominance over breadth.** SNAP doesn't try to win all of men's health — it wins **nitric oxide + prostate** specifically and has #1 positions in those sub-categories. Rootlab should likewise pick a 2–3-keyword category to genuinely dominate (likely "magnesium + ashwagandha" / "perimenopause sleep") rather than fighting on "wellness gummies" broadly.

## What Rootlab should NOT copy

- **The men's-health audience and creator vocabulary.** Different category language, different visual codes, different compliance pitfalls. Useful as inspiration on tactics, not on positioning.
- **Capsule format.** Rootlab's gummy is the audience-fit format; switching to capsules to chase a "more clinical" feel would forfeit the gummy-as-treat habit primitive.
