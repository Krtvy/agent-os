## D1 — Creator Program & Affiliate Design

- **Researchers and practitioners as partners, not endorsers.** Andrew Huberman, Stacy Sims, Andy Galpin, Tim Ferriss chose Momentous after finding it in pro-sports settings — not paid endorsements.
- **Co-developed protocols** with experts (e.g., "The Women's Three" with Dr. Stacy Sims). The expert is the product co-creator, not the spokesperson.
- **Distinctive:** This is a fundamentally different model than TikTok-Shop volume seeding. Trust comes from researcher endorsement rather than affiliate quantity. Operational lift is high (you have to find researchers willing to associate); brand equity is durable.

## D2 — Site & App Teardown

- 50+ SKUs organized by purpose (sleep stack, focus stack, women's protocol).
- **NSF Certified for Sport** prominently featured — only certification recognized by NFL/MLB.
- Subscription default; bundle-led PDPs.
- Education-heavy product copy (mechanism, sourcing origin, dosage rationale).

## D3 — Deals & Campaign Cadence

- Discount-light by category (premium pricing $40–70+ per SKU).
- Bundle pricing on "Momentous Three" is the primary AOV lift.
- USA Rugby launch (Jul 2025) — official-supplement-partner news cycle as marketing.
- Retail-expansion patience ("we've been able to say no until it's right" — same playbook as Raw Nutrition).

## D4 — Habit-Change Tactics

- **80/20 customer education philosophy:** focus consumers on the 20% of supplements that deliver 80% of value → reduces purchase regret + builds trust.
- Co-created **protocol-based** product framing (not "vitamin," but "Huberman's protocol") — turns supplement purchases into health protocols, shifting the mental model from discretionary to essential.
- Supply-chain transparency: Creapure (Germany) creatine, UK dairy whey, Norwegian omega-3 — explicit non-China sourcing as identity signal.

## What Rootlab should steal

1. **Co-developed expert protocol for Alpha GPC.** Find one credentialed expert (an RD, MD, or psychiatrist with academic credibility) and develop a "Rootlab Focus Protocol" or "Calm Sleep Protocol" co-branded with them. The co-creation reframes the SKU from "supplement" to "protocol" — higher trust, higher LTV, harder to commoditize.
2. **Supply-chain origin as PDP copy.** Where is Rootlab's KSM-66 ashwagandha sourced? Magnesium glycinate origin? Alpha GPC source? Make this explicit on every PDP. Even if the answers aren't the marketing-best (e.g., still China-sourced), the _transparency_ itself is a trust-builder.
3. **Bundle-as-protocol naming.** Momentous calls them "stacks" but frames them as "protocols." Rootlab's Sleep / Calm / Focus bundles should be reframed as "the [Expert Name] Sleep Protocol" once an expert partnership is in place — small naming shift, big mental-model shift.

## What Rootlab should NOT copy

- **The premium pricing tier.** Rootlab's gummy + TikTok-Shop channel won't sustain $40–70 SKUs. Momentous's pricing requires the elite-sports-credibility floor.
- **NSF certification right now.** Useful for elite-athlete positioning; expensive table-stakes for women's-wellness positioning. Defer until the brand competes for that audience.
