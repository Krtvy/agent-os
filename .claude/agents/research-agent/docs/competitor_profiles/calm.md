## What Rootlab should steal from Natural Vitality CALM

1. **Multi-format coverage of the same mechanism.** Natural Vitality has powder + gummy + chew of magnesium. Rootlab today is gummy-only on MagAshwa. A travel-sachet (powder) or chew SKU extension uses the same active ingredients to capture different occasion-fit consumers without splitting the brand.
2. **Retail launch as a credibility moat.** Even a single Sprouts or Whole Foods door creates the "as seen at..." asset that TikTok creators can film. Natural Vitality's 40-year retail presence is unattainable in a year, but the _retail-foothold-to-TikTok-credibility loop_ is.
3. **Own one word.** Natural Vitality owns "CALM." Bloom owns "Bloom." Olly owns benefit-words. Rootlab should pick one short, owned word ("Root," "Rest," "Restore"?) and make it the SKU-suffix branding across the line.

## What Rootlab should NOT copy

- **Retail-first distribution strategy.** Wrong moment in time. Natural Vitality built shelf presence over 40 years; Rootlab's leverage is creator-led TikTok velocity now, retail later.
- **Generic "magnesium" positioning.** Natural Vitality owns plain magnesium. Rootlab's edge is the **magnesium glycinate + KSM-66 ashwagandha** pairing — a more specific, differentiated mechanism story.

## Implications for the Calm Inc. (meditation app) thesis

The original profile's argument that "supplement-attached-to-app-habit is a structural moat" is still correct as a _general principle_ (Hims/Hers, Natural Cycles, Whoop), but **Calm Inc. doesn't currently sell its own supplement** — so the Calm-app brand is not a direct competitor on shelf. If Calm Inc. ever launches a supplement (likely candidate given $596M revenue + sleep brand equity), it would be a high-threat entrant. Add to monthly-snapshot watchlist.
