## What Rootlab should steal from MaryRuth's

1. **Build a "Credibility Playbook" creator artifact.** A one-page card bundled with every sample shipment: 5 banned terms, 5 safe equivalents, 5 hook frameworks (problem-agitate-solve, transformation, expert-stitch), 3 stitch-target categories. This is the single most directly stealable asset in the dataset and should ship in Rootlab's Phase-1 sprint.
2. **Time-bound + creator-paired drops over always-on discounting.** "48-hour exclusive on TikTok Shop" — coordinated with a wave of creator posts — generates spikes that train the algorithm and avoid teaching customers to wait for sales. Replace any always-on 10% off code with bursty creator drops.
3. **Bundle-and-sub checkout optimization.** Treat the PDP as a default-to-bundle / default-to-sub surface. MaryRuth's case study explicitly attributes growth to this. For Rootlab, the Sleep / Calm / Focus / Foundation bundles should be the default PDP entry point, not single SKUs.

## What Rootlab should NOT copy

- **Family-wellness positioning.** MaryRuth's owns the mom + kids + wellness vocabulary. Rootlab's audience is closer to "women managing their own stress" than "moms managing the family's wellness." Don't dilute the focus.
- **Liquid/dropper format.** Audience-fit different. Rootlab's gummy advantage is real.
