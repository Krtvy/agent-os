- **Compliance / brief structure:** unknown — but ACV claims are FTC-light (vs. cortisol/stress claims), so creators have wider creative latitude.

The combination of (a) 10→25% commission ladder unlocked at $5K GMV, (b) iPhone/retreat/BMW non-cash rewards for top performers, and (c) volume seeding is the most copy-able creator-program design in the dataset. ## What Rootlab should steal from Goli

1. **Counter-visible packaging design.** Goli's jars look at home next to a coffee machine and don't look like medicine. Rootlab's MagAshwa packaging should be evaluated against this benchmark — does it look like a wellness product or a pharmacy product? If the latter, it's a packaging refresh away from a habit-formation lift.
2. **Mass-retail-as-trust-signal scrolling press bar.** "As seen at Target / Walmart / Costco" treatment. Once Rootlab has even one retail door, that becomes a PDP credibility asset.
3. **Multi-pack pricing as the default PDP entry.** Goli's PDPs default-anchor to multi-packs (3-pack, 6-pack), not single jars. This is a free AOV lift — single SKUs as default is leaving 30%+ on the table per the bundling research.

## What Rootlab should NOT copy

- **The "candy" framing too aggressively.** ACV gummies are positioned as a soft-wellness habit; magnesium + ashwagandha need slightly more mechanism credibility (cortisol, sleep, perimenopause are reasoned-purchase decisions). Lean candy on aesthetic, but not on copy depth.
- **Mass discounting.** Goli's mass-retail and broad audience tolerate heavier promotional discounting. Rootlab's premium-gummy positioning probably can't sustain it without margin damage.
