# Habit-Change Tactics Inventory

> **Compiled:** 2026-05-06 · **Sourced from:** all 15 competitor profiles, `research_habits.md`, and the Rootlab growth playbook · **Purpose:** every observed packaging / app / lifecycle / content tactic across the dataset, tagged by brand and effect, organized so Rootlab can pick from a menu.

This is a _menu_, not a roadmap. Pick 3–5 high-leverage moves to implement; do not try to deploy all of them.

## 1. Format-as-habit (the structural primitives)

The dataset's strongest pattern: **the SKU format itself is the most durable habit tactic.** Lifecycle email is downstream.

| Tactic                                | Brand exemplar                     | Mechanism                                                                 | Rootlab fit                                                |
| ------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Single-serve sachet / drink stick** | Nello, Magic Mind                  | Eliminates measurement friction; binary done/not-done; travel-friendly    | High — MagAshwa travel-sachet line is a direct opportunity |
| **Pocket gum / mint format**          | Neuro                              | Situational use — bypasses "perceived optionality" trap of daily-dose     | Low — wrong format for magnesium/ashwagandha mechanism     |
| **Multi-gummy daily dose**            | Goli, Olly, Calm, MagAshwa current | Counter-visible, treat-coded; missing one gummy doesn't break the day     | Already in place                                           |
| **Powder + branded shaker**           | Bloom, AG1                         | Mixing ritual is filmable + makes consumption a UGC moment                | Low — different category                                   |
| **Refillable jar + monthly refills**  | Seed                               | Jar lives counter-side as artifact; refilling = brand re-engagement event | Medium — premium PM SKU candidate                          |
| **Clear bottle + clear capsule**      | Ritual                             | Visual transparency = daily compliance check (count remaining at glance)  | Low — gummy-format mismatch                                |

## 4. Identity framing (the language layer)

The Atomic-Habits / Clear-style identity-coding observed in the dataset.

| Identity frame                          | Brand      | Effect                                                               |
| --------------------------------------- | ---------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **"Foundational daily habit"**          | AG1        | Reframes from corrective to identity → +40% brand search             |
| **"I take Sleep" (benefit-named SKUs)** | Olly       | Identity-coded by product naming — "Sleep" not "Melatonin 5mg"       |
| **"Ritual" vocabulary**                 | Moon Juice | "Spirit Dust ritual," "Dream ritual" — pure ritual framing           |
| **"Bloom girl"**                        | Bloom      | Audience self-adopts identity marker                                 |
| **"Performance for life"**              | Momentous  | Identity-coded across customer life stages                           |
| **"Family caregiver"**                  | MaryRuth's | "I'm the one who keeps everyone healthy" — household-anchor identity |
| **"Future of vitamins is clear"**       | Ritual     | Identity = transparency-aligned consumer                             |
| **"Calm" (brand-as-identity)**          | Calm       | The brand name itself is the identity                                |

**Synthesis for Rootlab:** Most underused frame is "I take care of my nervous system." It's:

- Not owned by any brand in the dataset
- Mechanism-coherent across all three SKUs (magnesium = calm, ashwagandha = stress, Alpha GPC = nervous-system function)
- Identity-anchored vs. outcome-anchored
- Compatible with both perimenopause and broader wellness audiences

## What Rootlab should ship first (priority menu)

If you can only deploy 5 things from this inventory in the next 90 days, pick these:

1. **Daily-dose checkbox markers on the MagAshwa bottle label.** Zero cost on next print run; gamifies compliance.
2. **Pause-first cancellation flow with 5 reason-branched responses.** Saves 58% of cancellers per benchmark.
3. **6-touch onboarding email sequence with explicit "weeks 1–2 / 3–4 / 5–8" timeline.** +34% 90-day retention.
4. **Wallet pass with reorder timer + tier display.** +64% on-time reorders.
5. **Wellness profile quiz at first-purchase** (gated reward of points + personalized stack reasoning). Profile completers 2.7× LTV.

These five compound — none of them require new product, new packaging, or new infrastructure beyond what most subscription platforms (Recharge, Skio, Loop) already support.
