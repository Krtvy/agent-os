What the whole sheet does. A shared cross-POC operating tracker for Rootlabs' creator marketing. Every POC sees the whole thing (not just their slice). Answers four questions: how much money (per creator, per product, per video cohort), how is content production going (videos posted, new content driving revenue), what's each creator's state (active / silent / new / dormant), what's happening in operations (samples, deals, acquisition, reactivation). Data flows from Supabase (backend database) → Sheet → tabs that POCs consume. HGR is the hero product this month; MagAshwa is last month's launch tracked for momentum.

Foundation — the data everything else computes from

Raw receipts and registries. Most of these are appended from Supabase (Rootlabs' backend database). The Sheet is a downstream view; Supabase is source-of-truth. If a value looks wrong here, check Supabase first.

gmv_data (4,563 rows × 9 cols)

What it does. The receipts table. One row per sale event. Appended from Supabase.

Why it exists. This is the canonical revenue ledger for the month. Every dollar figure anywhere else in the workbook traces back to a row here. Synced from Rootlabs' Supabase backend — Sheet is downstream, Supabase is source of truth.

Locked videos (13 rows × 11 cols)

What it does. Videos that are private (TikToks set to private mode).

Why it exists. Private videos can't be public-attributed to GMV / public engagement. Tracker exists so the team knows which content is in a private state. ⚠ Confidence: medium — Kartavya wasn't 100% sure of the definition.

Operations — the verbs

What the team is actively DOING: acquiring new creators, reactivating dormant ones, shipping samples, signing paid deals. Samples are the unlock — creators need physical product before they can shoot a video.

Acquisition (48 rows × 20 cols)

What it does. New-creator pipeline tracking — for cold prospects we've never worked with.

Why it exists. The funnel for net-new creators: cold outreach → first contact → samples sent → first video posted → first sale. Distinct from Reactivation (which re-engages creators we've worked with before).

Reactivation (280 rows × 18 cols)

What it does. Bringing dormant (Silent) creators back into production.

Why it exists. Creators who used to work with Rootlabs and went silent. Reactivation is the re-engagement playbook — distinct from Acquisition which targets cold prospects.

Paid Deals (201 rows × 20 cols)

What it does. Quid-pro-quo contracts: "you make X videos, we pay you $Y."

Why it exists. Fixed-fee agreements (different from commission). Used when commission alone isn't enough to motivate a creator — typically for top-tier creators or to guarantee content volume during a launch push.

HGR Sample Delivered (0 rows × 0 cols)

What it does. Delivery confirmation log for HGR product samples.

Why it exists. Creators need the physical product to make videos. This tab is the source of truth for "has this creator received their sample?" If they haven't, low GMV from them isn't an under-performance signal — it's a logistics problem.

hgr_samples (284 rows × 10 cols)

What it does. Working queue for HGR sample logistics.

Why it exists. The verb side of sample management — pending shipments, request tracking, address handling. Pairs with HGR Sample Delivered: hgr_samples is "managing the process"; HGR Sample Delivered is "the resulting state."
