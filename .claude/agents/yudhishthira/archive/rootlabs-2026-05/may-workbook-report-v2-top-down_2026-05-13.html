What the whole sheet does (Level 1). The May workbook is a shared cross-POC operating tracker for Rootlabs' creator marketing. Every POC sees the whole thing, but each works their own slice. It answers four kinds of questions: how much money are we making (per creator, per product, per video cohort), how is content production going (videos posted, new content driving revenue), what's the state of each creator (active / silent / new / dormant), what's happening in operations (samples, deals, acquisition, reactivation). Plus tagging (POC ↔ creator), reference snapshots, and one external pull (EUKA).

Foundation — the data everything else computes from

Raw receipts and registries. Other tabs aggregate, filter, and present these. If something is wrong here, every downstream tab inherits the error.

gmv_data (4,563 rows × 9 cols)

What it does. The receipts table. One row per sale event.

Why it exists. This is the canonical revenue ledger for the month. Every dollar figure anywhere else in the workbook traces back to a row here.

Columns — what each tracks and why
ColumnWhat is being calculated/capturedWhy it matters
dateWhen the sale happenedFor attributing revenue to a specific day. Time-series tabs (DoD) slice by this.
creatorWhich creator (by username) made the saleThe unit of measurement. Every per-creator aggregation joins on this.
productWhich product was sold (hgr, alpha, magashwa, ppp, bb, confidence_duo, reset_trio, rl_seamoss, turmeric, glow_duo — 10 products)Different products are tracked separately. Main PoC, HGR tab, MagAshwa tab all filter by this. Most aggregations are product-scoped.
content_typeFormat of the content driving the sale (video, etc.)Different content types may need different attribution rules. Currently mostly "video".
content_idThe unique ID of the TikTok video that drove the saleThe atomic unit linking back to a specific piece of content. Video Data lookups happen on this. Per-video performance breakdowns groupby this.
post_dateWhen the content was postedMostly NaN in the data we have. ⚠ Pending confirmation — is this populated for some sources and not others? Or always empty?
traffic_typeWhere the sale traffic came from (organic, shop_ads, etc.)Distinguishes natural reach from paid-promoted reach. Lets the team see how much GMV is "earned" vs "bought".

Main PoC (827 rows × 2 cols)

What it does. The scoreboard every POC sees. 91 creators × 4 metrics, HGR-only.

Why it exists. Cross-POC visibility into who is doing well, who isn't, and where new content is driving fresh revenue. The single most-visited tab in the workbook.

Number of videosHow many HGR videos this creator posted in the periodContent production volume. A creator with high GMV but zero videos this period is earning from legacy content; a creator with many videos but low GMV is producing but not converting.
